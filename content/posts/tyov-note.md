While circling the Mediterranean sea in my travels, I spent some time in the Kamola region, a Roman province, and in my nightly revels, I organized a shady deal with some locals rogues who desired to disrupt the new construction of a bridge in place of an old aqueduct. The governess of the regions, one Yrabella Prazaria, would be forced to show her face and serve the people. In agreeing, I helped implode the bridge, and toppled several of the lady’s fish wagons. I was paid very well and was on the road soon after. 

![](/images/medusae01-0285.jpg.webp)

I was soon caught up with by the lady’s guards and brought before Prazaria herself. She’d been watching me and didn’t like the impediment to her bridge project I’d made happen. She had a look about her, and later, once she brought me to dine with her and her people, I soon realized I was among another immortal. No wonder the people never saw her in the daytime. If they did, she would send a body double that would hardly show her face, she told me. I was uneasy with such political intrigue and exposure and wanted to leave as soon as I could. She assured me that she would have eyes on me henceforth, no matter where my vagabond ways might lead me. 

I left in the dead of night, usually a time of newfound personal freedom for me of late, but had gained a new, and deep sense of paranoia upon leaving that time. I fear she cast some darker magic upon me in my stay at her castle.
